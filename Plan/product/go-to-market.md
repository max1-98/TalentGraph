# Product — Go-to-Market Strategy

## Cold-Start Problem

Two-sided networks fail at launch because neither side has reason to join
without the other. TalentGraph solves this by delivering **non-network value
first**: tools that are useful to a candidate with zero employers on the
platform.

### Phase 1 — Individual Tools (single-player mode)
Deploy gap analysis, CV creation, and career pathing as standalone tools.
A candidate who uploads their CV receives immediately useful output.
No employer presence required. No outreach. Pure information value.

This phase builds a candidate base with real, enriched profiles.

### Phase 2 — Employer Invite (seeded network)
Invite a small number of employers (one per target role cluster initially)
to post roles. The algorithm runs against the existing candidate pool.
First matches are generated. Candidates receive their first real outreach
notifications.

Employers see a curated pool of pre-interested candidates; candidates see
their first match. Both sides get proof of value at the same moment.

### Phase 3 — Two-Sided Growth
Network effects engage. Employers invite colleagues. Candidates refer peers.
The feed and community layer activates: skill trend signals, peer moves,
industry insights. The platform becomes a professional destination, not just
a job alert tool.

---

## Atomic Network Model

A professional network needs density before it functions. The atomic unit
is not a city or a country — it is a **professional community cluster**: a
role type within an industry vertical within a geography.

Seed one such cluster completely before expanding. Within that cluster, the
match quality is high because the candidate pool is deep. Employers get
results. Candidates get relevant matches.

Do not dilute by expanding too early.

---

## Single-Industry Focus Rationale

Starting industry-agnostic dilutes match quality (the candidate pool is thin
everywhere) and brand identity (the platform is "for everyone" which means
nothing to anyone).

Owning one vertical first means:
- Deep candidate pool in one domain → better match quality → better employer
  outcomes → word-of-mouth growth
- Concentrated skill taxonomy coverage in that domain
- Clear positioning: "the platform for [X] professionals"

The choice of initial vertical is a separate commercial decision. This doc
is industry-agnostic by design — the model applies to any vertical.

---

## Expansion Playbook (template)

1. Select next vertical based on: adjacency to current (skills transfer),
   employer demand signal, candidate volume potential
2. Seed candidate side first using individual tools (same Phase 1 approach)
3. Run cross-vertical match at launch: some employers want mixed profiles
4. Maintain separate community feeds per vertical; allow cross-vertical
   discovery as an opt-in setting
5. Repeat

---

## Candidate Acquisition Levers

- SEO-driven tools: "free CV analyser", "skill gap checker for [role]" — high
  intent, top-of-funnel
- Referral: "invite a colleague to see their career profile"
- Content: industry insight posts, skill trend reports (feed layer)
- Partnership: bootcamps, universities, professional associations

## Employer Acquisition Levers

- Direct outreach to hiring managers in target vertical once candidate pool
  reaches threshold density
- Free first hire: no fee until a match leads to a shortlist
- Platform-generated insight reports: "here's what the candidate market looks
  like for your next role" (lead generation tool)
